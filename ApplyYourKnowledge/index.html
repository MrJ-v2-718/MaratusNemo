When planning a website you must first determine the purpose of the site as well as the audience. You should also take into consideration variances in screen size and shape as well as different browser types. The structure of the site should be decided as well, but webbed structures tend to be the most common. Alternatives are linear and hierarchical (ordered and directory based). This is also where you decide which graphics to use, the font and size, color, and any search bars. Last but not least, you should design your website to be accessible to everyone, this means accomodating for any type of visual or hearing impairments.

Chapter 1 taught me how to plan for the design of a website, the basics of the internet and browsers, some standard protocols used on said internet, internet vs. extranet vs. intranet, as well as HTML basics and implementation.
